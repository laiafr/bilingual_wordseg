Project Description

This directory contains the transcripts from the single Spanish-speaking child in the Serra / Solé longitudinal study of 10 children. In the larger study, five children are monolingual Catalan, four are bilingual Catalan-Spanish, and one is monolingual Spanish. The Spanish speaking-child, Eduard, was videotaped from 1;4 to 3;10 from 1986 to 1989. He was videotaped at his homes in spontaneous interaction with his mother, in sessions of 30 to 45 minutes. The family was middle-class.

The research project is entitled “Language acquisition in Catalan and Spanish children” and is directed by Miquel Serra (Universitat de Barcelona) and Rosa Solé (Universitat Autonoma de Barcelona). It has received support from the Spanish research council (Grants DGICYT PB84/0455; PB89/0317; PB91/0851; PB94/0886). The research assistants of the project have been: Montserrat Cortés, Connie Schultz, Elisabet Serrat, Vicens Torrens, and Melina Aparici. Cristina Vila and Montse Capdevila have collaborated in different stages of it.
